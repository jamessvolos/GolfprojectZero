Templates  /  Redan

Template

Redan

North Berwick (West Links), 15th — Scotland

The most-copied hole in golf: a green set on a diagonal shelf, tilting away from the player front-right to back-left, guarded short-left by a deep bunker. It punishes nothing and tempts everything — the shot that looks like a bail-out is the one the ground rewards.

The strategic idea

A Redan asks one question: attack the flag through the air, or use the ground? The direct shot must be flown the full distance and held on a surface running away from it — low percentage. Land it short and right, seemingly bailing out, and the slope feeds the ball toward the hidden flag. The Redan rewards the golfer who aims away from the target and trusts the contour to finish.

The Strategic Line  /  Notes

The design language

Green, parchment & brass

Dark-academia green as the foundation — the tone of library baize and bottle glass — kept quiet, with parchment for reading and a single gilt accent. Walnut, leather and oxblood appear only in small warm doses. A deliberately single, aged world.

Restraint

  • Green leads. The dark surfaces are bottle green, not wood; walnut and leather are accents, not the room.
  • Hairlines, not hardware. A single 1px brass rule replaces chunky beams and shiny fittings.
  • One accent. Brass, used sparingly — rules, numerals, the line of play.
  • Quiet motion. Slow fades and a view-transition crossfade; nothing that announces itself.

Diagrams & data

  • Hole schematics as small framed prints on a green ground — a gold dashed line for the intended play.
  • Tables as plain catalogues: a brass small-caps header over hairline rows, figures in tabular numerals.
  • Fidelity as brass pips — analytical, but quiet.

Photography direction

  • Low, warm, lamp-lit light — dusk and interiors. Push toward green-gold; let shadows go deep.
  • Archival images as framed prints: a thin brass mat, a little grain, an engraved caption.
  • Course photography in rich, shallow focus — the ground and its contours, never the sky.
