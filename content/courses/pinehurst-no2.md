Pinehurst No. 2 is the clearest classroom for Donald Ross's particular idea of
strategy: the trouble is at the green, not off the tee. The fairways are
generous; the crowned, turtleback greens are merciless. Miss on the wrong side
and the ball trickles thirty yards down a false front into a collection area,
leaving a delicate pitch or putt back up the slope.

The Coore & Crenshaw restoration stripped away decades of green rough and
returned the sandy, natural waste ground, restoring both the look and the
options Ross intended. It is the modern revival and the Golden Age shaking
hands — a strategic ideal recovered rather than reinvented.
