Chicago Golf Club was the first 18-hole course in America and a charter member
of the USGA — Macdonald's home club and one of his earliest canvases. The
course as played today is largely Seth Raynor's 1923 redesign, a superb
inland set of template holes: a Redan, a Biarritz, a Short, and Raynor's
signature squared, boldly tilted greens.

It earns its place on the map as the Midwestern anchor of the Macdonald–
Raynor school and a demonstration that the templates were never coastal
curiosities. Given firm ground and clever routing, the same strategic ideas
that were born on Scottish links translate perfectly to the Illinois prairie.
