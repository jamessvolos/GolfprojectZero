Riviera is George Thomas's argument in built form: that the soul of the game
is strategy, and that the architect's job is to keep offering the player a
meaningful choice. Kikuyu grass, a barranca winding through the property, and
some of the most intelligent green complexes in America make it a course that
rewards thought over power.

Two holes are required reading. The par-3 6th has a bunker set *inside* the
green, splitting it and forcing the player to choose a side. The par-4 10th is
one of the greatest short holes in golf — drivable, but to a tiny angled green
where the aggressive line brings a card-wrecking miss into play. Both are
pure Thomas: the reward for boldness is real, and so is the price.
