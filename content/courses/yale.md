Yale Golf Course is the most dramatic of the American template courses — a
muscular, hard-rock layout carved out of Connecticut woodland that takes the
Macdonald–Raynor vocabulary and renders it at enormous scale. It was built for
the university on land donated for the purpose, and its template holes are
outsized versions of the Scottish originals.

Its 9th is the most celebrated Biarritz in the world: a ferocious long
one-shotter across a pond to a huge green split by a swale deep enough to
stand in. The 4th is a stern Redan. For the study of templates, Yale is
essential — proof that the same portable ideas that began on gentle links
could be amplified into something monumental without losing the decision at
their core.
