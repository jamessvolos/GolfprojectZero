TPC Sawgrass is Pete Dye's manifesto of manufactured drama, carved out of a
Florida swamp and shaped into the first purpose-built stadium course. It is
proof that heroic design, done with intelligence, can still pose real
strategic questions rather than merely frightening the player.

The island-green 17th is the most famous short hole in the world — a wedge to a
green surrounded by water, with no bailout and nowhere to hide. It is a
provocation, and a divisive one, but it belongs in the strategic conversation:
it strips a golf decision down to its barest terms — commit fully or fail —
and in doing so shows the emotional stakes that the subtler templates dress up.
