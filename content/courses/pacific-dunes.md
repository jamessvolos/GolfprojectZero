Pacific Dunes is the course that proved the minimalist revival could produce a
world-ranked design and not just a nostalgic exercise. Doak routed it through
existing Oregon duneland, moving almost no earth, and let the ground supply the
strategy — firm turf, wide fairways, blind shots, and an unorthodox routing
with back-to-back par 3s and a stretch of consecutive par 5s.

It belongs on this map as the contemporary answer to North Berwick: a links
built a century and an ocean apart, working from the same conviction that the
best holes are found in the land and ask a question rather than issue a
sentence. Where the National assembled strategy from ideas, Pacific Dunes
recovered it from the ground.
