Prestwick hosted the first Open Championship in 1860 and the first twelve
thereafter. It is golf in its wildest early grammar: blind carries over dunes,
the vast sleepered wall of the Cardinal, and greens tucked into hollows you
cannot see until you crest the ridge.

The 17th, the **Alps**, is the template's origin — a long approach played
blind over a towering dune ridge to a green hidden behind, with the deep Sahara
bunker waiting across the front for anyone who comes up short of the carry. It
is the archetype of the "hit it and hope" hole redeemed by strategy: the player
who takes on the full carry from the correct angle is rewarded with a sight of
the green that the bailout never earns.
