Fishers Island is the course Raynor admirers reach for when they want to argue
he was the equal of any architect of the age. Routed around coves and bluffs on
a private island, it sets the Macdonald–Raynor templates against some of the
finest coastal ground in American golf.

For this study it is a rich seam of instances: a Redan, a Biarritz, and a
Punchbowl all sit within one routing, letting you compare Raynor's handling of
the same archetypes he built elsewhere against a completely different and more
dramatic landscape. It is the coastal counterpart to inland Camargo — the same
vocabulary, a wilder stage.
