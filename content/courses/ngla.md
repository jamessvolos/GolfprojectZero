The National is the founding document of American strategic architecture — a
course conceived not as an original composition but as a deliberate anthology
of the best holes in the world, rebuilt on the sandy Long Island coast.
Macdonald and Raynor gave nearly every hole a template lineage: a Redan, an
Alps, an Eden, a Sahara, a Short, a Cape, a Bottle.

Because it was assembled from ideas rather than found in the land, the National
is the ideal course for studying templates as a *system*. You can walk from a
Redan to a Cape to a Short in an afternoon and see the same designer render
four completely different decisions — proof of Macdonald's thesis that a great
hole is a portable argument about risk and reward.
