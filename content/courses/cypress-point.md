Cypress Point is the course MacKenzie's admirers reach for when they want to
prove his thirteen principles were more than theory. It moves through three
distinct landscapes — inland forest, sand dunes, and the ocean cliffs — and
finds strategic golf in each, culminating in the famous par-3 16th, a
do-or-die carry across the Pacific with a safe route left for anyone unwilling
to take the ocean on.

That 16th is the perfect distillation of the site's thesis. It is not a penal
hole; it is a **choice**, offered honestly and in full view. The bold line and
the safe line are both playable, and the design simply makes the reward
proportional to the risk. MacKenzie would have asked for nothing more.
