Augusta National was conceived by MacKenzie and Bobby Jones as an inland
homage to the Old Course: wide, firm, and full of options, with few bunkers
and enormous, wildly contoured greens where the real defense lived. The
original had almost no rough; strategy came from angle and slope, not
punishment.

The modern Augusta is a more heroic, more manicured place than MacKenzie drew
— but the strategic DNA survives most vividly at the 13th, the par-5 turn of
Amen Corner, where Rae's Creek fronts the green and the entire hole is built
around a single question: do you go for it in two, or lay up? It is one of the
best risk-reward decisions in championship golf, and it is featured in full on
Page 2.
