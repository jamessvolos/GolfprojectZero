Sand Hills is the course that launched the destination-minimalism movement.
Coore & Crenshaw walked the vast prairie dune site for years, plotting
candidate holes on foot, and in the end built a course that feels entirely
discovered — as if the holes had always been there and someone simply mowed
them.

Its place on this map is as the American frontier of the strategic revival:
enormous width, firm and fast ground, and options everywhere, played across a
landscape that had never seen golf. It is the clearest modern proof of the
Golden Age lesson that great strategy comes from the right land read the right
way, not from earthmoving budgets.
