Biarritz Golf Club, on the French Basque coast, is on this map chiefly for what
is *no longer there*. Its long par-3 "Chasm" hole — with an approach carried
across a gully to a green fronted by a deep swale — so impressed the young
C.B. Macdonald that he carried the idea home and made it a staple template.

The original hole is gone, altered out of existence, which makes Biarritz a
useful cautionary entry: the template outlived its origin. Every Biarritz hole
played today — at the National, at Yale, at Fishers Island — descends from a
hole you can no longer stand on. It is the clearest case of Macdonald's
central claim that a hole is fundamentally an *idea*, portable and survivable
apart from its ground.
