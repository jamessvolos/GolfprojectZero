North Berwick is the great living museum of strategic golf. Nothing here was
theorized into place; the links simply accreted around the folds of the East
Lothian coast, walls and burns and blind shots and all. It is the source
material — the course Macdonald and his contemporaries studied to extract the
ideas they would later carry across the Atlantic.

The 15th, the Redan, is the most copied hole in golf, and to stand on its tee
is to understand why: the green sits on a diagonal shelf, falling away from
the player, guarded short-left by a deep bunker, and the whole design invites
a running shot fed from the right. It is the purest instance of the site's
thesis — a hole that offers a route, not an ultimatum.
