If strategic golf has a home ground, this is it. The Old Course is a lesson in
width as a design tool: fairways so broad that the trouble is not *whether* you
can find grass but *which* grass leaves the shot you want. The double greens,
the diagonal hazards, the maddening Road bunker — all of it rewards the player
who thinks two shots ahead and punishes the one who merely aims at the flag.

Two of the game's foundational templates were born here. The 11th, the **Eden**,
is the model short one-shotter played to a green slung between bunkers and the
estuary behind. The 17th, the **Road Hole**, is the most famous strategic hole
in golf: a tee shot that dares you to cut the corner over the hotel and a
second to a sliver of green defended by the Road bunker in front and the road
and wall behind.
