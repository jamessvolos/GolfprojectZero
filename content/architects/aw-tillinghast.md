A.W. Tillinghast — "Tillie the Terror" — was a Philadelphia dandy who turned a
private obsession with golf into a body of work that still hosts major
championships. Winged Foot, Baltusrol, San Francisco Golf Club, Bethpage
Black: courses that ask big, muscular questions and do not much care whether
you are ready for them.

Tillinghast leaned heroic. His holes tend to demand a carry, a commitment, a
willingness to take the shot on rather than route around it. But he was too
good to be merely penal — his green complexes give the bold player somewhere
to aim and the cautious player a longer, safer road to the same place. His
"Great Hazard" bunkering and his fearsome one-shotters are the work of a man
who believed a championship hole should frighten you a little.
