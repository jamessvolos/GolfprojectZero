Gil Hanse spent the early part of his career as the restoration specialist the
great old clubs called when they wanted their MacKenzie or Ross or Raynor
brought back rather than modernized. That deep apprenticeship in the Golden
Age masters — reading their intentions from the ground up — is what he now
brings to original work.

Hanse won the commission for the 2016 Rio Olympic course and has since become
the most sought-after name in the game, with new builds and sympathetic
restorations at Los Angeles Country Club, Winged Foot, Merion, and Pinehurst.
He works in the minimalist tradition, on-site with a shovel as often as a
plan, but his restorations are the more important legacy: a generation of
strategic architecture, blurred by decades of well-meaning "improvements," is
being returned to the choices its designers intended.
