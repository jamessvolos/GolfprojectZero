Charles Blair Macdonald is the reason this site has a Page 1. Schooled at St
Andrews in the 1870s, he returned to America convinced that the great British
holes were not accidents but **ideas** — the Redan, the Alps, the Eden, the
Road — each a self-contained argument about risk and reward that could be
studied, abstracted, and rebuilt anywhere.

At the National Golf Links of America (1911) he did exactly that: a course
assembled deliberately from the best holes in the world, reinterpreted for
Long Island. It was the first serious attempt to treat design as a
**classification system** rather than a craft passed hand to hand. Macdonald
was imperious, litigious, and often insufferable — but he gave the discipline
its grammar. Every "template hole" in the game traces back to his conviction
that a good hole is a portable decision.
