Macdonald coined the "Cape" concept for a hole where the target sits out over a
hazard on a diagonal, letting the player choose exactly how much to attempt.
The most celebrated version is a driving hole — a fairway or green thrust out
over water so that the aggressive line, carrying more of the hazard, is
rewarded with a shorter or better-angled next shot.

The Cape is the template that most literally embodies strategic design's
central claim. It does not tell you where to hit it; it draws a diagonal across
your options and prices every yard of courage. A timid player and a bold player
can both make par, but they will take completely different routes, and the
design makes the trade honest. If you want to explain risk and reward to
someone in one hole, you show them a Cape.
