The Short is Macdonald and Raynor's demonstration that a hole barely a wedge
long can still pose a genuine question. The green is large and heavily bunkered
all the way around, with a pronounced internal contour — most famously a
horseshoe ridge — carving it into distinct sections.

Because the carry is trivial, all the strategy migrates to the green itself.
The pin sits in one quadrant; the ridge stands between you and the others. Play
boldly to the correct section and a birdie is there; misjudge it and you are
either in a bunker or facing a putt that swings feet across the horseshoe. The
Short is the antidote to the idea that championship golf must be long golf —
it is all touch, nerve, and reading the internal contour, on a hole you could
almost throw a ball across.
