The 11th on the Old Course — the "High (In)" hole, universally known as the
Eden after the estuary behind it — is the template for the demanding short
one-shotter. Its green falls steeply from back to front and is guarded by two
of the most famous bunkers in golf: the small, savage Strath short-right and
the Hill bunker short-left.

What makes it a template rather than merely a hard hole is the way the defenses
interact with the tilt. Every pin position redefines the choice. The front
surface repels anything not perfectly judged; go long to be safe and you face a
downhill putt that can trickle off the front. Bobby Jones famously picked up
here in frustration in 1921 before falling in love with the course. Macdonald
and Raynor reproduced the Eden widely, always preserving that essential
tension: a short shot where the safe play barely exists.
