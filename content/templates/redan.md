The Redan is the most copied hole in golf, and the template that best captures
what this whole site is about. The name comes from a fortification — a V-shaped
redan angled to the attacker — and the golf hole borrows the geometry: a green
set on a diagonal shelf, defended short-left by a deep bunker, falling away
from the player from front-right to back-left.

Its genius is that it punishes nothing and tempts everything. The player who
fires straight at a back-left pin brings the bunker and the runaway slope into
play; the player who lands short and right, seemingly bailing out, watches the
ball trundle down the contour to the heart of the green. The correct shot
looks like a miss. Every serious template architect built one — Macdonald,
Raynor, and MacKenzie chief among them — because it is the definitive proof
that a hole can offer a route rather than issue a sentence.
