The 17th at St Andrews is widely held to be the hardest par 4 in championship
golf and the finest strategic hole ever to evolve by accident. The tee shot
plays over the corner of the Old Course Hotel — historically the black railway
sheds — and the golfer must decide how much of that corner to carry. Cut off
more and you shorten the hole and open the angle; cut off less and you leave a
long approach from the wrong side.

That approach is the stuff of nightmares: a green angled away from the player,
scarcely more than a few paces deep in places, with the notorious Road bunker
gouged into its front-left shoulder and the road and wall pressed against its
back edge. Bail out right and you may find the road; go for the flag and the
bunker swallows you. The Road Hole is the template of consequence — the hole
that made "playing to the fat of the green" a strategy worth a bogey.
