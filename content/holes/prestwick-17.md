The 17th at Prestwick is the origin of the Alps template — a long approach
played completely blind over a huge dune ridge to a green concealed behind it,
with the deep Sahara cross-bunker waiting across the front for anything short
of the full carry. You aim at a marker on the crest, commit, and wait to top
the hill and learn your fate. It is the archetype of strategy under
uncertainty: the courage to hit hard at a target you cannot see.
