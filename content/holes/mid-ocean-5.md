The 5th at Mid Ocean is the Cape decision rendered as clearly as the template
ever gets. A par 4 curling around a lagoon, it hands the golfer a dial: bite
off as much of the diagonal water carry as you dare from the tee, and be
rewarded with a short pitch from a fine angle; bail out left to safety and the
hole lengthens as the angle sours. There is no forced answer, only an honest,
beautifully staged trade between courage and position — which is exactly why
this hole, more than any other, made the Cape famous.
