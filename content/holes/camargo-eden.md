Camargo's Eden takes the demanding St Andrews short one-shotter inland and
renders it in Raynor's bold, geometric idiom: a green falling steeply from back
to front, guarded by fierce flanking bunkers, so that every pin re-poses the
question of which miss you can live with. It is a key member of Camargo's
almost-complete par-3 set and a fine study in how the Eden survives far from
its estuary origin.
