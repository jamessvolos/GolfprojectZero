The 16th at the National carries Macdonald's Punchbowl — a green cradled in a
hollow, its surrounding slopes feeding shots back toward the middle. After the
sterner templates it plays as a deliberate change of pace: the bowl is
forgiving, the approach only semi-blind, and the strategy migrates to
controlling position *within* the gather so as not to leave a long, curling
putt. Proof that the Macdonald vocabulary included generosity as well as
fear.
