Yale's 9th is the Biarritz raised to its most monumental. A long one-shotter
played across water to a vast green split by a swale of genuinely startling
depth, it takes the idea Macdonald carried home from France and renders it at
a scale the original never had. The choice is the Biarritz choice — fly the
full carry to the back plateau, or land short and trust the ground — but here
the stakes are magnified by the pond in front and the sheer size of everything
beyond it. It is the template's definitive American statement.
