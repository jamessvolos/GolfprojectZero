Chicago Golf Club's Biarritz is a prime example of the template carried inland
by Seth Raynor's 1923 redesign — a long, exacting one-shotter to a big, narrow
green bisected by a deep swale and guarded by bunkers on both flanks. It shows
that the Biarritz idea, born on the French Basque coast and orphaned when its
origin hole was lost, translates perfectly to a firm prairie course: the same
choice between an all-carry aerial shot and a lower running one fed through the
swale.
