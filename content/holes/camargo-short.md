Camargo's Short is a benchmark of the template: a wedge to a big, boldly
contoured green encircled by bunkers, where the trivial carry gives way to all
the strategy migrating onto the putting surface. The horseshoe ridge sets the
pin apart from the rest of the green, so the choice is pure precision versus
position. It is the reason Camargo is so often cited when the Short template is
discussed.
