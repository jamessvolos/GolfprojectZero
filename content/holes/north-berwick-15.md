The 15th at North Berwick is where this site's thesis stops being an argument
and becomes a place you can stand. There is no forced carry, no water, no
penal framing — just a green tilted and angled so cleverly that the safest
route and the most rewarding route are, remarkably, the same shot: the running
ball fed off the front-right that the slope delivers to a hidden flag.

Play it once and you understand why Macdonald spent his life trying to bottle
it. The Redan does not punish the golfer who aims at the flag; it simply
offers a better idea, in full view, and lets the ground reward anyone wise
enough to take it.
