The 3rd at the National is Macdonald's rebuilt Alps, carrying the blind-approach
idea across the Atlantic. The second shot is played over a fronting ridge to a
green hidden behind, with a deep cross-bunker guarding anything left short. It
is a faithful reproduction of the Prestwick drama on Long Island sand — proof
that the "hit it and hope" hole could be transplanted as an idea and still ask
its original question.
