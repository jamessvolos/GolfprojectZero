The 6th at the National is the archetypal Short — a wedge to a big green
encircled almost entirely by deep bunkers and carved by a pronounced internal
horseshoe ridge. The carry is trivial and the strategy is all in the green:
the flag sits in one quadrant, the ridge stands between you and the others, and
the choice is pure precision versus position. It is Macdonald's proof that a
hole barely a wedge long can still pose a genuine question.
