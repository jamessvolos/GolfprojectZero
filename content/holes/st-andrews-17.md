The Road Hole is the most studied strategic hole on earth, and the one that
most vividly makes this site's point about the *linked* decision. The choice
does not live in a single shot; it is a strand of risk running from the tee to
the green, where every yard of courage over the hotel corner is repaid — or
punished — on the approach to that impossible sliver of green.

Its enduring lesson is that laying up, playing away from the flag, and
accepting a bogey can be the highest expression of strategic intelligence. The
Road Hole made "the fat of the green" a phrase worth a dropped shot, and in
doing so it taught the whole game that the smart play and the brave play are
not always the same.
