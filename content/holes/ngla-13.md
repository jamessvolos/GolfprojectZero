The 13th at the National is Macdonald's Eden, transplanting the demanding short
one-shotter from St Andrews to Long Island. It keeps the essential ingredients:
a green tilted back-to-front and pinched between deep bunkers in the Strath and
Hill positions, so that every pin redefines which miss the player can live with.
A faithful reproduction of the Old Course's 11th, minus only the estuary
waiting behind.
