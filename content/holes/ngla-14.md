The Cape at the National is the hole to show someone when you want to explain,
in ninety seconds, what strategic golf architecture *is*. Macdonald built no
single correct line into it. He drew a diagonal across the player's options and
priced every yard of courage, then stepped back and let each golfer set their
own risk tolerance.

It is the site's thesis rendered as a single continuous line of ground. There
is no trick and no forced answer — only an honest trade, offered in full view,
between the yards you are willing to carry and the shot you will have left. Bold
and timid players walk off with the same par by completely different routes,
and the design makes the bargain fair to both.
