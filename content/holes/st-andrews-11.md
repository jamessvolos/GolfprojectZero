The 11th on the Old Course is the model for one of golf's hardest short
one-shotters. Its green falls steeply from back to front and is defended by two
of the most famous bunkers in the game — the small, savage Strath short-right
and the Hill short-left. There is almost no safe middle: every pin turns the
hole into a different negotiation between the bunkers, the tilt, and the
trouble beyond.
